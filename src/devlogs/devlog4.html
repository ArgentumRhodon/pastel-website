Devlog 4: MAGIC & More

MAGIC Maker Application

It’s been really fun seeing our basic systems and improvements culminating in an actual game that people have fun with. We’ve decided that, although the application itself is a requirement of the class in which this project is being developed, we would like to apply for and pursue the MAGIC Maker Program, which provides students with some funding and support for the development of their digital media projects at a prototyping, production, or publishing level.

If approved, Team Pastel would go into the prototyping phase, further polishing and iterating on our game until we found a good balance and feel. We feel prototyping would be best at this stage because of the big changes our overall level layouts and mechanics are seeing. Production would be best for the content generation that comes after our prototype feels solid.

Two Sprints Left!

Seeing our game come together in a more formal sense with the MAGIC Maker application was rewarding. With our core gameplay features solidified and only two sprints remaining, we’ve decided to move on to polishing existing facets of the game and iterating and expanding on our level designs. Although Mixbox is an excellent tool that saved us a lot of development time, its color outputs are only an approximation, meaning some shades of yellow and blue work better than others for creating an appealing green. So, in the interest of polish, a web tool for fine-tuning color palettes was developed. Our level designer made use of this tool to find colors that would create easily distinguishable shades and mixes while also maximizing the aesthetic appeal.

Identity to Community

As part of our process, we expected and were prepared for things to change. But we didn’t expect just how constantly our game would morph into what it’s become. Our second playtest rendered feedback suggesting our game lent itself more toward helping and valuing community and others than discovering one’s own identity. Given this shift, our experience goal of instilling pastel emotions also shifted to a more wholesome theme, and our target audience expanded to include both college students and those with familial and community-focused values.
