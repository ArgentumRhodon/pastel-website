Devlog 1: Pre-Class Discussions

A New World

In the beginning, there was only darkness. But then there wasn’t, and what would become Team Pastel began its long journey, chopping and stomping and slowly navigating their way through the darkness in an attempt to bring light, meaning, and emotion – and, soon enough, slimes – to this dark world. Our first step along this path was answering the question of “What do we want?”

Establishing Expectations

As game developers, it's important to establish what we want out of our projects. Answering questions like: What are our individual goals for this project? What do we want going in? What do we want coming out? Are there certain skills we wish to improve? What does the team as a whole want for the project? What sorts of priorities do we have? Answering these questions helps the team set an initial approach to the project.

Our initial meeting broadly answered these questions on a team and individual basis. For brevity, individual goals will be withheld, but our team’s Core Ideals were established as such: The project should be challenging enough to keep members engaged and passionate about the project, but our approach and process should utilize limited meetings and regular code freezes to keep crunch to a minimum. Our method should also follow lean practices, only adding processes to solve active problems.

Targeting an Audience

Once general project expectations have been set, ideation begins! What do we want in our dark world? Who do we want to see what we’ve discovered? What makes them interested? How will it affect them? Our next meeting focused on answering these questions at least a little bit.

When considering who you want to target, it's important to think about what audiences are accessible to your team. Making sure your target audience is an accessible resource for playtesting ensures that you can make accurately informed decisions on what turns to take during development.

After extensive consideration defining experience goals, demographics, themes meaningful to the team, and more, we landed on a theme of discovering identity and the personal process and emotions involved. We found a target audience of college students to best fit our requirement for relevance to the theme and ease of access for playtests.

That’s It!

This concludes the first devlog, in which we explored our opening strategy for exploring a dark unknown world: establishing team expectations, target theme, and target audience.
